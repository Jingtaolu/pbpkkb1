Marvin JS : Drawing Graphical Objects

Boxes

In Marvin JS, you have the ability to draw different graphical objects such as a rectangle or an ellipse. To draw a shape, you can use the Graphical Object tool on the Tools toolbar. This is a "combo " button; you can choose the appropriate shape by left-clicking on the black triangle on the lower right corner of the button.

You can use these shapes as frames for your structures. In order to distinguish the frames, the color of the background and the line is changeable by opening the graphical objects pop-up menu.


You can easily resize your structure: Left-click on one of the control points of the rectangle/ellipse and, without releasing the mouse button, drag the line to the right place.

Snapped objects

You have the ability to assign arrows to the control point of the shapes. It means that when you drag the shape, the arrow will be pointing to the same control point on the dragged object. This feature can be useful especially when one wants to highlight the steps of a mechanism.

It can be made by creation or by dragging the end of the arrow. 

You will not be able to rotate the frame in 2D or 3D; however, you will be able to move the whole structure by rotation. See the molecule transformations here.

When you want to disconnect the arrows from the boxes, you just need to drag the end of the arrow away from the box.
Erasing the box results that the attached arrow will remain on the canvas, pointing to its original direction.
